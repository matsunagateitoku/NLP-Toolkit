Common Dependency Relations:

nsubj - Nominal subject (who/what does the action)
dobj - Direct object (receives the action)
amod - Adjectival modifier (adj describing noun)
advmod - Adverbial modifier (adv modifying verb/adj)
det - Determiner (the, a, an)
prep - Prepositional modifier (in, on, at)
pobj - Object of preposition
aux - Auxiliary verb (is, has, will)
cc - Coordinating conjunction (and, but, or)
conj - Conjunct (word joined by conjunction)
ROOT - Root of the sentence (main verb)
compound - Compound word parts
poss - Possessive modifier ('s, of)
neg - Negation (not, never)
punct - Punctuation
attr - Attribute (complement of copula)

Example:

"The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog."

  • fox is the nsubj (subject) of jumps
  • quick is an amod (modifier) of fox
  • over is a prep (preposition) attached to jumps
  • dog is the pobj (object of preposition) of over
